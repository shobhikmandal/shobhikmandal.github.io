Our solar system consists of eight planets that revolve around the Sun, which is central to our solar system. These planets have been classified into two categories that are "inner planets" and "outer planets". Mercury, Venus, Earth and Mars are known as the "inner planets" or "Terrestial planets". Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus and Neptune are know as the "outer planets" or the "Gas giants".

Around 13.8 billion years ago, all the matter in the Universe emerged from a single , minute point in a violent burst. This expanded at an astonishingly high rate and temperature, doubling in size every 10-34 seconds, creating space as it rapidly inflated. Within a tiny fraction of a second gravity and all the other forces were formed. Energy changed into particles of matter and antimatter, which largely destroyed each other.

After the Big Bang, the universe was like a hot soup of particles Over billions of years, gravity caused gas and dust to form galaxies, stars , planets, and more. The matter that spread out from the Big Bang developed into everything in the universe, including us. Astronomers have figured out that the universe is about 13 billion years old.
