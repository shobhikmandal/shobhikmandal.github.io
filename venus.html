Venus




Facts

• Venus is the second planet from the Sun.
It is the second largest terrestrial planet.


• It is named after the Roman goddess of love and beauty.
It is sometimes referred to as the Earth’s sister planet due the their similar size and mass.


• Venus is the second planet from the Sun.
It is the second brightest object in the night sky after the Moon.


• Venus is sometimes referred to as the “morning star” and “evening star”.
The Moon is the only other natural object that is brighter.


• The planet has an apparent magnitude of -3.8 to -4.6, which makes it visible on a bright, clear day.
Venus is the sixth largest planet.


• Venus' orbit is the most nearly circular of that of any planet;
It has an eccentricity of less than 1%


• The diameter of Venus is 12,103.6 km (7,520.8 mi)—only 638.4 km (396.7 mi) less than Earth's
It's mass is about 81.5% of Earth's mass.


• Average Temperature on Each Planet Mercury – 800°F (430°C) during the day, -290°F (-180°C) at night.
Venus – 880°F (471°C) Earth – 61°F (16°C).
