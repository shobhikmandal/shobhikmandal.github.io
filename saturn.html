Saturn




Facts

• Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun and last of the planets known to ancient civilizations.
Saturn is one of five planets able to be seen with the unaided eye.


• In Roman mythology Saturn was the father of Jupiter, king of the gods.
Saturn was known to the ancients, including the Babylonians and Far Eastern observers.


• Saturn’s upper atmosphere is divided into bands of clouds.
Saturn orbits the Sun once every 29.4 Earth years.


• Saturn is the flattest planet.
Saturn has oval-shaped storms similar to Jupiter’s


• Saturn is flattened at the poles, due to a fast rotation on its axis.
Saturn was the god of agriculture in Roman mythology.


• Saturn is a gas-giant planet and therefore does not have a solid surface like Earth’s. But it might have a solid core somewhere in there.
Saturn's atmosphere is made up mostly of hydrogen (H2) and helium (He).


• Saturn orbits at a distance of about 886 million miles (1.4 billion kilometers) from the Sun.
Nine Earths side by side would almost span Saturn’s diameter.


• Saturn cannot support life as we know it, but some of Saturn's moons have conditions that might support life.
Saturn is also regarded as it has 53 known moons with an additional 29 moons awaiting confirmation of their discovery.So there is a total of 82 moons on Saturn.
