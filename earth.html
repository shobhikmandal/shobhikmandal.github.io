Earth




Facts



• The gravity between the Earth and the Moon causes the tides on Earth. Earth is the only planet not named for a mythological god or goddess.

• Earth is the most dense planet in the solar system. The Earth was once believed to be the centre of the universe.

• The rotation of the Earth is gradually slowing down. The Earth was formed approximately 4.54 billion years ago and is the only known planet to support life.

• Earth is the third planet from the Sun It is also the largest of the terrestrial planets.

• The Earth is the densest planet in the Solar System. The Earth’s atmosphere is composed mainly of nitrogen (78%), oxygen (21%), argon (.93%), and carbon dioxide (0.03%).

• Earth is the only place in the solar system where water can be present in all the three states – solid, liquid and gas. The existence of water in the liquid phase on Earth is due to the temperature span that exists on the Earth: 0 – 100 degree Celsius.

• The Earth is traveling around the Sun at an average velocity of 107,182 kilometers per hour. And at the same time, we are also spinning with an enormous speed.

• It is gravitationally locked and this rotation is unique to the solar system. Earth's inner and outer core are estimated at 6,000 C*.
